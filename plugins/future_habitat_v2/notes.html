Data Notes

Current Tidal Marsh Areas: Maine Natural Areas Program, Maine Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry 2015. Locations of tidal marshes in Maine as mapped by the Maine Natural Areas Program, Maine Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry. Data are mapped at 1:24K or larger scale. The minimum mapping unit for tidal marshes was 5 acres though most, but not all marshes between 2.5 and 5 acres were also included. For more information: http://www.maine.gov/dacf/mnap/assistance/tidal_marshes.htm
Future Habitat / Marsh Migration Data: Maine Natural Areas Program, Maine Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry 2015. Low lying areas of the non-tidal landscape that are adjacent to tidal estuaries that could be inundated at highest annual tide if sea level is increased by 1 foot, 2 feet, 3.3 feet, and 6 feet. These are areas where existing tidal marshes could migrate or expand given corresponding increases in sea level. This data covers the entire Maine coastline. For more information: http://www.maine.gov/dacf/mnap/assistance/marsh_migration.htm
Parcel Information: Maine Office of GIS. Organized Townships – 2016, Unorganized Townships – 2014. For Organized Townships, displayed Parcel Name is combination of Town Name and Map-Book-Lot (where available). For Unorganized Townships, displayed Parcel Name is combination of Town Name and GPL code. For more information: http://www.maine.gov/megis/catalog/
Road-Stream Crossings: The Nature Conservancy in Maine and The Gulf of Maine Coastal Program, U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service 2017 et al. Latest (2017 release) version of field-surveyed Road Stream crossings classified as ‘Barriers’ or ‘Potential Barriers’ to aquatic organism passage on Public roads. For more information: https://www1.maine.gov/dacf/mcp/environment/streamviewer/
Non-Tidal Wetlands: Non-tidal, freshwater wetlands extracted from the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service National Wetlands Inventory geodatabase data and the USFWS Hydric soils 2007 geodatabase. The full dataset represents the extent, approximate location and type of wetlands and deep-water habitats in the conterminous United States. For more information: https://www.fws.gov/wetlands/FAQs.html
Regions: The Nature Conservancy in Maine 2017. The Maine Coastal Zone divided into regions based on a modified version of TNC’s Northwest Atlantic Marine Ecoregional Assessment Coastal Assessment Framework (CAF), a watershed-based spatial framework used to organize and classify coastal and marine assessment information.
Current Conservation Lands: The Nature Conservancy 2014. Public and private lands and waters secured for conservation. In this web map, lands are symbolized by Gap Status (Level of Protection). For more information: http://www.conservationgateway.org/ConservationByGeography/NorthAmerica/UnitedStates/edc/reportsdata/terrestrial/secured/Pages/default.aspx
Wildlife and Habitat Concentrations: Maine Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife 2017. Concentration of select wildlife and wildlife habitat values on the landscape. Currently available for Hancock and Washington Counties only. For more information: http://www.beginningwithhabitat.org
High-Resolution Topography (LIDAR): Maine Office of GIS 2013. A hillshade portrayal of a 2m x 2m Lidar-based Digital Elevation Model (DEM). For more information: http://www.maine.gov/megis/catalog/
